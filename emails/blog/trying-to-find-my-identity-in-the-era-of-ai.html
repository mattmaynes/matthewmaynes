Trying to Find My Identity in the Era of AI
For all of my career I prided myself on my craft: clean abstractions, low coupling, the boring discipline that makes software easy to change later. Then AI arrived, I went all in, and a big chunk of what I thought was the job quietly stopped mattering. What followed was an honest identity crisis, and then a second one when the token budget ran dry and I realized I had let the machine write code I no longer understood.
This is where I am now: my old craft feels dead, velocity is the new currency, and I tipped too far into delivery before a respected peer reminded me what I had traded away. I don't have the balance figured out yet, but I'm trying to find it, and this post is me thinking it through in the open.
